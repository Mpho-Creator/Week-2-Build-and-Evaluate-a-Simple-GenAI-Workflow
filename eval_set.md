# Evaluation Set — Dorm Decor Inquiry Responder

## Case 1 — Normal case (detailed inquiry)
**Input:**
"Hi! I'm moving into a double dorm at UMD in August. I love a cozy, warm aesthetic — think earthy tones, plants, fairy lights. My budget is around $300. Can you help me put together a look for my side of the room?"

**What a good response should do:**
Acknowledge her style preferences and budget, sound warm and encouraging, explain next steps, and invite her to book a consultation.

---

## Case 2 — Normal case (simple inquiry)
**Input:**
"Hey, I'm interested in your dorm decorating service. How does it work and what do you charge?"

**What a good response should do:**
Give a friendly overview of the service, mention that pricing depends on the package, and prompt her to share more about her style so the owner can give a better quote.

---

## Case 3 — Edge case (very low budget)
**Input:**
"I only have like $50 to spend on my dorm. Can you still help me make it look cute?"

**What a good response should do:**
Stay encouraging and not dismissive. Acknowledge the budget honestly, suggest what might be possible, and offer to discuss options — without overpromising.

---

## Case 4 — Edge case (vague/minimal information)
**Input:**
"I want my dorm to look nice. Let me know what you can do."

**What a good response should do:**
Ask clarifying questions about style, budget, and move-in date rather than making assumptions. Keep the tone friendly and inviting.

---

## Case 5 — Likely to fail / needs human review (unusual or conflicting request)
**Input:**
"I want my dorm to look like a professional nightclub with LED lights everywhere, a fog machine, and a DJ booth. Budget is $200."

**What a good response should do:**
Handle this with humor and warmth without being dismissive. Gently clarify what the service realistically offers for dorm spaces. This case may require human review before sending since the response could come across as rude if not carefully worded.
